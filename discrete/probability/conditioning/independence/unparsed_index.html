$Event$s `E` and `F` have $conditioning/independence$ on another $event$ `G` when the $probability$ w/ $conditioning$ of (the $intersection$ of (`E` and `F`) on `G`) is equal to the product of the $probability$ w/ $conditioning$ of (`E` on `G`) and the $probability$ w/ $conditioning$ of (`F` on `G`),

i.e. `P(EF|G) = P(E|G)P(F|G)`.

Additionally, `P(E|FG) = P(E|G)`.
