1. The $probability$ of the null set is 0.
  2. $probability/axiom$ 3 works for finitely many sets.
  3. The $probability$ of the $complement$ of an $event$ is 1 minus the $probability$ of that $event$ (non-complement).
    i.e. `P(bbb"E"^c) = 1 - P(bbb"E")`.
  4. The $probability$ of an $event$ is less then or equal to the $probability$ of any of it's supersets.
    i.e. if `bbb"E" sube bbb"F"` then `P(bbb"E") <= P(bbb"F")`.
  5. The $probability$ of the $union$ of two $event$s is the sum of their probabilities, minus the $probability$ of their $intersection$.
